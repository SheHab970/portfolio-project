Questions & Answers

What services do you offer?

I specialize in backend engineering: API design & implementation (REST, GraphQL, gRPC), microservices and service-oriented architectures, server-side application development, database schema design and migrations, background jobs and queueing, authentication/authorization, caching strategies, performance tuning, and production-grade monitoring and observability.

How do you approach a new project

I begin with discovery and requirements gathering, then produce a lightweight architecture and data model that balances scalability, reliability, and cost. I define API contracts (OpenAPI/GraphQL schema), choose an appropriate stack, and break work into iterative milestones with automated tests and CI/CD. I prioritize small, demonstrable deliveries and keep communication channels open for feedback and reviews.

What is your project timeline?

Timelines vary by scope. Typical examples: a single API endpoint or small feature can take 1–2 weeks; a complete backend for a medium web app (auth, core APIs, DB) is usually 4–8 weeks; large systems or microservice platforms take longer and are planned in phases. I provide a milestone-based schedule with estimated delivery dates after the discovery phase.

How much do your services cost?

I offer hourly, fixed-price for well-scoped projects, and retainer options for ongoing maintenance. Costs depend on scope, integrations, compliance requirements, and uptime/SLA needs. After an initial brief I provide a transparent proposal with line-item estimates and optional support plans.

What platforms do you work with?

I work across common backend stacks and cloud platforms: .Net and Java. Datastores include PostgreSQL, MySQL, Redis, and other NoSQL solutions. I use Docker, Kubernetes, serverless (AWS Lambda/GCP Cloud Functions), and CI/CD tooling (GitHub Actions, GitLab CI). I also integrate message queues (RabbitMQ, Kafka) and monitoring (Prometheus, Grafana, Sentry).
